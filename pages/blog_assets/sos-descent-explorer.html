Geometry of numbers · Gaussian integers

The shortest vector is the answer.

Every prime p ≡ 1 (mod 4) is a sum of two squares. Here is how to find them: the numbers (a,b) with a ≡ r·b (mod p) form a lattice, and its shortest nonzero vector (a,b) satisfies a² + b² = p. Watch the Euclidean algorithm in ℤ[i] descend to it — every step a nearest-lattice-point hop.
