One square, three languages

This 4-cycle is the ℓ = 2 isogeny graph of the order of discriminant −39. It is simultaneously a Cayley graph of the class group ℤ/4, a fusion graph of its representations, and a system of particles. Every vertex wears all three labels at once.

D = −39  ·  h = 4  ·  Cl = ℤ/4  ·  each edge = one 2-isogeny = ×[𝔩]±1 = fuse with χ ⊕ χ̄

The mirror runs along the real axis (vertices 0 and 2). Reflecting across it swaps 1 ↔ 3 and fixes 0 and 2 — this single involution is complex conjugation, Frobenius, and charge conjugation, all at once. Press it and watch the graph.

Class group

Vertices are elements of Cl = ℤ/4. An edge is multiplication by the prime class [𝔩] above 2 — but you can't tell [𝔩] from [𝔩]⁻¹, so the edge is undirected.

Representation

Vertices are irreps χ ∈ Hom(ℤ/4, ℂ*). An edge is fusion with the distinguished real 2-dim irrep χ ⊕ χ̄ — undirected precisely because that rep is self-dual.

Particle

Vertices are particle species; an edge fuses with a self-conjugate probe. Filled = matter, hollow = antimatter. The two rose nodes are their own antiparticles.

The two real j-invariants (−3.32×10⁸, −190.23) are the 2-torsion classes A[2] = the self-dual irreps = the particles equal to their own antiparticle. The complex pair 744 ± 18197 i is the conjugate pair {[𝔩], [𝔩]⁻¹}. A spanning set recovers the group up to that invisible mirror; a rigid spanning set chooses a side of it.
